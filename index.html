Answer these questions and check your health status for coronavirus.

    1 Potential Risk

    Testing is currently focused on individuals who have traveled outside Sri Lanka or had contact with someone diagnosed as having COVID-19, or have developed symptoms of COVID-19.

    However, there’s a chance you could get sick since it’s less than 14 days since your exposure. You should self-isolate until 14 days after the exposure.

    • Please do not visit a hospital, physician’s office, lab or healthcare facility without consulting 1999 or your district MOH office.
    • Don't go to any public places, stay at home, and don’t have any visitors.
    • Don’t share personal items like dishes, cups, or towels and wash your hands often.
    • If you do develop any Fever or Dry Cough or Difficulty in Breathing, take this self-assessment again or Call 1999 or your district MOH office immediately.

    If you are experiencing other symptoms like Tiredness, Aches and Pains, Nasal Congestion, Runny Nose, Sore Throat, Diarrhoea and want assessment contact: 1999 or your district MOH Office immediately

    2 Medium Risk

    Based on the response you have provided, please call1999 or your district MOH Office immediately.

    Because you have ( or had ) symptoms, you need to self-quarantine until medical services reach you.

    • Please do not visit a hospital, physician’s office, lab or healthcare facility without consulting 1999 or your district MOH office.
    • Don't go to any public places, stay at home, and don’t have any visitors.
    • Don’t share personal items like dishes, cups, or towels and wash your hands often.
    • If you do need emergency medical assistance call 1999 and let them know you may have COVID-19

    3 High Risk Tier 1

    Contact 1999 immediately, Avoid using public transportation to reach medical assistance, avoid close contact with anyone, Wear a face mask or cover your mouth and nose with some form of thick fabric, wash your hands with soap every 30 minutes or use alcohol based sanitizer until medical assistance gets to you. Read our self-quarantine guide.

    4 High Risk Tier 2

    Contact 1999 or Your district MOH Office immediately for assistance in assessing your condition, Avoid using public transportation to reach medical assistance, avoid close contact with anyone, Wear a face mask or cover your mouth and nose with some form of thick fabric, wash your hands with soap every 30 minutes or use alcohol based sanitizer until medical assistance gets to you. Read our self-quarantine guide.

    5 High Risk Tier 3

    Contact 1999 or Your district MOH Office immediately for assistance in assessing your condition, Avoid using public transportation to reach medical assistance, avoid close contact with anyone, Wear a face mask or cover your mouth and nose with some form of thick fabric, wash your hands with soap every 30 minutes or use alcohol based sanitizer until medical assistance gets to you. Read our self-quarantine guide.

    Testing is currently focused on individuals who have traveled outside Sri Lanka or had contact with someone diagnosed as having COVID-19, or have developed symptoms of COVID-19.
